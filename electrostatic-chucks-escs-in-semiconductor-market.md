<p><strong>The Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) in Semiconductor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Coulomb Type</li><li>Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are essential in the semiconductor market for holding substrates during processing. The Coulomb type ESC utilizes electrostatic forces to cling to the substrate, ensuring stability and precision during operations. Conversely, the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type ESC uses a combination of electrostatic forces and induced polarization, providing enhanced clamping efficiency and reduced particle generation. Both types are critical in improving yield and performance in semiconductor fabrication processes, catering to diverse requirements in advanced manufacturing.</p></p>
