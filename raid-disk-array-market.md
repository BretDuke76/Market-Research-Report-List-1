<p><p>The RAID disk array market is categorized into several types based on performance, redundancy, and data protection. RAID 0 offers high speed through striping but lacks redundancy. RAID 1 mirrors data for redundancy but reduces capacity. RAID 5 combines striping and parity, providing a balance of performance and fault tolerance. RAID 6 enhances RAID 5 with double parity for extra safety. RAID 10 merges mirroring and striping for high performance and redundancy. RAID 50 and others further refine these approaches to meet specific enterprise needs.</p></p>
